# Research Basis

Use these sources as evidence boundaries, not rigid scripts. Behavioral studies support conversation principles; surveys and app guides provide Japan-market heuristics but do not guarantee outcomes for an individual match.

## Conversation Quality

- Huang et al., *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* (2017): follow-up questions convey responsiveness and were associated with greater liking; in speed dating, more follow-up questions predicted more second-date agreement. Apply as `acknowledge her answer -> ask one connected follow-up`, not `ask many questions`. https://doi.org/10.1037/pspi0000097
- Sprecher et al., *Journal of Experimental Social Psychology* (2013): turn-taking reciprocal self-disclosure produced greater liking, closeness, similarity, and enjoyment than one-sided disclosure. Apply as matched-depth, alternating disclosure rather than monologues. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jesp.2013.03.017
- Kashian et al., *Computers in Human Behavior* (2017): online self-disclosure can increase liking through interpersonal attributions, while overly intimate disclosure from a stranger can create negative attributions. Apply as gradual depth: routine/preferences first, vulnerable material later. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chb.2017.01.041

## Japan-Market Timing

- PR Times survey of 238 experienced users: use only as a descriptive LINE-timing pattern, not a universal threshold. https://prtimes.jp/main/html/rd/p/000000003.000015190.html
- IBJ Match Lab: treat LINE exchange around practical date coordination as safer than an immediate request. https://www.ibjapan.jp/match-lab/contact-exchange/
- mybest message guide: use profile/common-interest topics, readable length, and frequency calibrated to the other person. https://my-best.com/articles/1866
- Dine guide: simple questions in days 1-3, a date bridge around the first week when interaction is warm, LINE after planning, and a low-pressure lunch/public first meeting. These are editorial heuristics and signals override elapsed time. https://dineapp.co.jp/dating-howlong/
- Amie dating coach: ask specific open-ended questions and tie an invitation to the active topic. https://amiethedatingcoach.com/how-and-when-to-ask-someone-out-from-a-dating-app/

## Safety

- Japan National Police Agency: early migration from a matching app to LINE can be a romance-fraud warning; impersonation can be assisted by translation tools or generative AI; requests involving investment, marriage costs, or money should trigger suspicion. https://www.npa.go.jp/bureau/safetylife/sos47/case/sns-romance/romance/

Operational rule: screen authenticity first, keep suspicious contacts in-app, never follow money/investment instructions, and recommend block/report when high-risk signals appear.
